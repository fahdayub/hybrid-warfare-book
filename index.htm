This book was transformed

from a doctoral thesis, and effectively

communicates Pakistan’s security perspective.

 

The book comprises authentic research work reinforced by a comprehensive bibliography

(citing around 100x primary sources & 515x secondary sources) along with reviews from well-known personalities in diplomatic, military, academic and think tanks.

The names include Pakistan's former Chairman Joint Chiefs of Staff Committee General Zubair Mahmood Hayat, AJ&K's former President - Ambassador Sardar Masood Khan, Pakistan's former Foreign Secretary and High Commissioner to New Delhi Ambassador Sohail Mahmood and Dr Asma Shakir Khawaja former Dean National Defence University.

Based on a PhD thesis, the research work has undergone requisite academic scrutiny from Pakistani and foreign supervisors.

Moreover, its concrete theoretical framework along with the synthesis between realism and

constructivism leading to an innovative ideation of neo-classical realist constructivism lends it

academic credence for wider acceptance in educational and intellectual circles.

The book covers latest developments in Pakistan-India security milieu including a dedicated chapter on Pakistan’s victory in Marka-e-Haq, explaining how the Armed Forces of Pakistan

maintain a superior edge over the adversary. Pakistan’s role in arriving at a peace deal in Gaza in

concert with other Muslim countries is highlighted as a dividend of Pakistan’s elevated stature after

Marka-e-Haq. Pakistan’s recent mediation efforts between Iran and USA to achieve lasting peace in the Middle East are a sequel to Pakistan’s global acceptance as a credible geopolitical player.

The book provides a wide spectrum of interesting/ educative material for various brackets of

readers based on their levels of education and intellect:-

- Descriptive - Historical facts to formulate the education base of young readers

- Exploratory - Examination of disputed facts related to national sovereignty

- Investigative - Exposing India’s diplomatic, economic & informational onslaught

- Analytic - Impact on Pakistan’s national security in all DIMEFIL domains

- Future projection - Causal relationship between security dilemma and security spiral to forecast events impacting regional stability

- Theoretical contribution - Academically innovative synthesis of realism with constructivism coining a new theoretical concept

Foregoing, broad dissemination of the book will help spread a narrative aligned with Pakistan’s national security to fill the vacuum arising from non-availability of such material at national and international level.
